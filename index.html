A Life Well Lived

A family built with purpose, and a bass line that never stopped.

Gamer at 67

Russell is an avid Guild Wars player with multiple accounts and no intention of slowing down. He has always had a knack for finding things people don't expect from him, and gaming is no exception. When the next entry drops, he'll be ready.

Family First

The job provided security and stability, but family was always the reason behind the work. Russell made it his goal to be present — active in his children's and grandchildren's lives.

Live and Let Live

Russell's philosophy is straightforward: go be the best version of yourself you can be, and leave everyone else to do the same. No titles, no ideology, no noise. Just show up, do the work, and don't give the people who oppose you any bandwidth to occupy.

The Man Behind the Bass Line

Some people collect stamps. Russell delivered them — for over three decades, through heat and rain, up long driveways and across quiet suburban streets, arriving not just as a mail carrier but as a familiar face people were genuinely glad to see.

But the postal uniform was only one chapter. Behind it, for as long as he can remember, there was a bass guitar. Russell picked it up at sixteen with paper-route money, taught himself to play, and never put it down. Today he plays bass for Hagatha’s Bluff, a local band built on good music and honest friendship.

“Never walk away from something you love. Even when you’re down, don’t abandon the things that make up who you are.”

This is the story of an ordinary life lived extraordinarily well — on foot, on stage, and on his own terms.
