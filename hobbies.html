Music & Hobbies

Bass Guitar · Hagatha’s Bluff · Life in Tune

The Bass Holds Everything Together

For Russell, the bass guitar has never been just an instrument — it has been a philosophy. Nobody at a show talks about the bass player. They feel it in their chest and can’t quite explain why the song sounds so right. That invisibility, that quiet foundation-work, is exactly what drew him in.

He picked up his first bass at sixteen with money saved from a paper route, taught himself to play from scratch, and has been playing ever since. More than fifty years later, the bass is still the first thing he reaches for when he needs to say something that words won’t cover.

“Bass kinda just picked me, and I stayed with it because I liked it.”

Hagatha’s Bluff

The band that has been a part of Russell's musical journey.

The Band

Russell found Hagatha’s Bluff through Band Mix — a message he almost ignored. The woman behind it had a professionally recorded CD done, and when he heard it, he knew this was something worth auditioning for. He’s been their bassist for about a year now, and the fit has been right from the start.

The Instrument

A bass guitar tells the story of who has played it. Every scratch and worn fret is a record of time spent. Russell’s bass has been with him through decades of sets, rehearsals, and late-night jams — an object that carries the weight of everything he’s put into it.

Monday Nights

Hagatha’s Bluff rehearses most Monday nights. Someone brings snacks, someone brings opinions. They run through the set, argue about tempo, and usually end up jamming on something unplanned. Those unplanned moments, Russell will tell you, are the best ones.

Beyond the Stage

Music is the headline, but it isn’t the whole story.

Retirement

Retirement gave him back the one thing a long career quietly takes: time that belongs entirely to him. He spent thirty years delivering for everyone else. Now he gets to deliver for himself.

Family & Grandbabies

Russell had made a decision somewhere along the way that mattered more than any other: to show up. Not just physically, but truly present — engaged, active, and invested in the lives of his children and grandchildren in a way that left no doubt he was there.

Guild Wars

At 67, Russell is an avid video gamer — multiple accounts, years of play, and genuinely looking forward to the next entry. He has always had a knack for finding things people don’t expect from him.
